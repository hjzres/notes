1.0 General Review


Equations of Lines

The equation of a line can be expressed in the following forms

  1. The slope-y-intercept form, y = mx + b eg. y = 3x + 4
  2. General form, ax + by = c e.g. 3x + 4y = 12
  3. Standard form, Ax + By + C = 0 e.g. 3x + 4y - 12 = 0

There are also two special uses that include horizontal lines of the form y=b and vertical lines of form x = a

Properties of Parabolas

Certain properties of a quadratic relation help to describe it. The definitions of these properties are listed below

Vertex: The highest or lowest point on the parabola that intersects the axis of symetry

Axis of Symetry: Vertical lines that divide the parabola into congruent halves

Optimal Value: y-coordinate of the vertex

x-intercept(zeros): point(s) on the parabola that intersects the x-axis

y-intercept: point on the parabola that intersects the y-axis

Equation of Parabolas

The equation of a parabola can be expressed in three different forms

  1. Vertex form, y = a(x-h)² + k e.g. y = 3(x - 4)² + 5
  2. factored form, y = a(x - r)(x - s) e.g. y = 3(x - 4)(x + 5)
  3. Standard form, y = 3x² - 4x + 5 e.g. y = 3x² - 4x + 5

The sign of "a" indicates the direction of opening. If "a" is positive the parabola opens up and if "a" is negative, the parabola opens down.

The Factored Form

To determine the vertex of a parabola factored form, y = a(x - 3)(x - s), follow the steps below.

  1. Determine the zeros.
  2. Determine the x-coordinate of the vertex, xv, by taking the average of the zeros.
  3. Determine the y-coordiate of the vertex, yv, sub xv into the equation and evaluate

The Standard Form

To determine the vertex of a parabola from Standard form, y = ax² + bx + c, follow the steps below

  1. Determine the x-coordinate of the vertex, xv, using xv = -b/2a
  2. Determine the y-coordinate of the vertex, yv, by subbing xv into the equation

The Vertex Form of a Quadratic Relation

When the equation of a parabola is in vertex form, y = a(x - h)² + k, the coordinate of the vertex is (h, k)

y = 3(x + 4)² + 5
y = 3(x - (-4))² + 5
h, k = (-4, 5)
